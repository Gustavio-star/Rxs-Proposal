The City

About Roxas City

The Seafood Capital of the Philippines, in the heart of Capiz.

The Seafood Capital

Roxas City is the capital of Capiz province in Region VI (Western Visayas). With a population of over 185,000, it is the most populous city in Capiz. Known as the Seafood Capital of the Philippines, the city is celebrated for its rich marine resources — crabs, lobsters, mussels, oysters, and the prized diwal (angel wings clam).

History & Heritage

Originally named the Municipality of Capiz, the city was chartered on May 12, 1951, and renamed in honor of Manuel Acuña Roxas — the first president of the independent Third Philippine Republic and a native son of the city. Heritage structures including the Immaculate Conception Metropolitan Cathedral (c. 1870s) still stand in active use today.

Economy & Progress

Roxas City is the center of trade, commerce, and logistics in Northern Panay. The city's economy is anchored in aquaculture — particularly oyster and milkfish farming — alongside a growing commercial sector. Daily flights connect to Manila via Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and AirAsia.

Discover

Visit Roxas City

Pristine coastlines, heritage landmarks, world-class seafood, and a city that truly welcomes you.

Beach
Baybay Beach

Gray-sand beach along the Sibuyan Sea, lined with fresh seafood restaurants and pavilions. The crown jewel of Roxas City.

Ecotourism
Mantalinga Island

A pristine island off the coast of Roxas City with clear waters and white-sand shores — perfect for a day escape from the city.

Island Hopping
Olotayan Island

A short boat ride from Baybay Beach — pristine waters, coral gardens, and white-sand shores perfect for snorkeling.

Culinary
Seafood Capital

Fresh oysters, crabs, lobsters, and the rare diwal (angel wings clam) — served open-air at the city seafood court.

Heritage
Immaculate Conception Cathedral

Built in the 1870s — one of the oldest Spanish-era churches in Panay and a landmark of Capiz heritage.

Festival
Halaran Festival

Celebrated every October — grandest thanksgiving festival of Capiz, with street dancing, agri-trade fairs, and cultural shows.
